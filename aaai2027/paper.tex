\begin{abstract}
Offline safe reinforcement learning methods often select deployment actions by thresholding learned cost or feasibility scores.
Even when these scores admit marginal conformal calibration over candidate proposals, deployment-time action selection can concentrate calibration errors among the actions that are actually certified and executed.
We study this calibration-after-selection problem for offline safe RL under moving budget queries.
The intended contribution is not another constraint-adaptive policy optimizer, but a selection-aware auditor that targets false certification among issued action claims for a declared proposal stream, continuation policy, and support condition.
The first required evidence is an exact counterexample showing that ordinary global or group conformal calibration can remain nominal over proposals while failing after action selection.
\end{abstract}
